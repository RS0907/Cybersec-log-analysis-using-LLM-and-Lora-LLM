CHAPTER 1
INTRODUCTION
1.1 BACKGROUND
In the current digital age, organizations have become reliant on networked systems,
cloud platforms, and distributed infrastructures in their day-to-day operations. Huge
volumesofsecuritylogsareproducedbyfirewalls,intrusiondetectionsystems,servers,
and applications each second by these systems. These logs will carry valuable data re-
gardingactivitiesofthesystems,userusage, andpotentialsecurityrisks. Nevertheless,
the process of going through such huge amount of data manually is very hard, time
consumingandinmostcases,itresultsininaccuracies.
Conventional security monitoring platforms are primarily based on pre-established
rules and well-known attack signatures to detect threats. Although these systems are
effectiveincaseofknownattacks,theyusuallyfailincaseofnewordevelopingattack
patterns. This limitation has resulted in the popularization of the use of the Machine
Learning(ML)techniquesintheautomaticidentificationofabnormalpatternsandma-
liciousactivitiesinthelogdata.
Most machine learning models are black boxes, even though they can enhance the
detection accuracy. They give forecasts, but they do not make it clear why this or
that log or activity was considered suspicious. This complicates the trust that security
analysts have to the system and may slow down the process of making decisions in a
criticalincident.
WiththeblisteringdevelopmentofArtificialIntelligence(AI)andparticularlyLarge
LanguageModels(LLMs),ithasbecomepossibletomakethemmorecomprehensible.
LLM is able to produce human readable descriptions of identified threats, enabling
analysts to comprehend the problem and respond more quickly. A smarter and more
feasible cybersecurity solution can be developed by combining the reasoning and ex-
planationaspectsofLLMswiththedetectionabilityofmachinelearning.
1
1.1.1 SecurityLogAnalysisChallenges
Security logs are often unstructured, voluminous, and generated from a variety of
heterogeneous sources. This makes preprocessing and normalization difficult. In addi-
tion, distinguishing between normal and malicious behavior requires identifying subtle
patternsthatareoftenhardtodetectusingconventionalmethods.
1.1.2 ImportanceofMachineLearningandLLMs
Machine learning models such as Random Forest and XGBoost can effectively clas-
sify log entries based on learned patterns. However, these models often lack inter-
pretability. LLMs, on the other hand, are capable of understanding context and gen-
erating natural language explanations. By integrating both approaches, the system can
not only detect threats but also explain them in a way that is understandable to human
analysts.
1.2 MOTIVATION
This project is motivated by the increasing complexity of cyber threats and the limita-
tions of current security analysis techniques. As organizations continuously generate
massive amounts of log data, the need for intelligent systems capable of both detecting
andexplainingthreatsisgrowingrapidly.
• Problem Significance: Cyber attacks are becoming increasingly sophisticated,
requiringmoreadvancedmethodstoidentifythem.
• Practical Motivation: Security analysts require faster and more interpretable
insightsforeffectiveresponse.
• Academic Motivation: The application of ML and LLMs in cybersecurity is an
emergingfieldofresearch.
• Technical Interest: Combining structured ML models with unstructured lan-
guagemodelsistechnicallyinteresting.
• Aspect of Innovation: Adding an explanation layer with LLMs makes the sys-
temmoretransparentanduser-friendly.
2
1.3 SCOPEOFTHEPROJECT
This project aims to design and evaluate a hybrid cybersecurity log analysis system
usingmachinelearning-baseddetectionandLLM-basedexplanations.
• FunctionalScope:
– Processingandanalyzingsecuritylogdatasets.
– Binaryclassificationoflogentries(attackvsnormal).
– Generatinghuman-readableexplanationsofdetectedevents.
• Non-FunctionalScope:
– Ensuringhighaccuracyandlowfalsepositives.
– Improvingusabilityandinterpretability.
– Supportingprocessingoflarge-scaledata.
• TechnicalScope:
– ImplementationusingPython,Scikit-learn,andXGBoost.
– Explanationgenerationusingpre-trainedorfine-tunedLLMs.
– Evaluationusingstandardperformancemetrics.
• ProjectBoundaries:
– Thesystemcurrentlysupportsonlyofflineanalysisofhistoricaldata.
– Real-timedeploymentandautomatedresponsemechanismsarenotincluded.
– Assumestheavailabilityoflabeleddataforsupervisedlearning.
– Doesnotaddressadvancedadversarialattackscenarios.

2.1 LITERATUREREVIEW
Analysis of cybersecurity logs has gained increased significance with the fast-paced
development of networked systems and digital infrastructures. Conventional intrusion
detection systems were rule-based systems, which worked well to find out known at-
tacksbutcouldnotidentifynewandemergingthreats. Thisshortcomingresultedinthe
useofmachinelearning(ML)anddeeplearning(DL)methods.
Machinelearningmodels(RandomForest,SupportVectorMachinesandXGBoost)
have been shown to perform quite well in intrusion detection with supervision. Never-
theless,thesemodelsareveryreliantonlabeleddataandcannotbeinterpreted.
Unsupervisedmethods,suchasIsolationForestandAutoencoders,areabletoiden-
tifyunknownattacks,buttendtohavelargefalsepositiverates.
The latest developments have brought Large Language Models (LLMs) to cyberse-
curity. LLMs are able to process logs and provide human-readable explanations. Nev-
ertheless,adaptationmightberequiredtoensurethatpre-trainedLLMscanbeeffective
indomain-specifictaskslikeanalyzingcybersecuritylogs.
To manage this, recent studies consider parameter-efficient methods of fine-tuning
likeLoRA(Low-RankAdaptation),thatenablesadaptationofLLMstodomain-specific
datausingalowamountofcomputation. Thisstrategyenhancesperformanceandrele-
vancewithouthavingtore-traintheentiremodels.

2.4 PROBLEMSTATEMENT
The current cybersecurity systems produce large amounts of log data, and it is not fea-
sible to analyze it manually. Although machine learning models can identify malicious
activity, they are not interpretable. Pre-trained LLMs are not domain-specific but have
anexplanation.
The purpose of this project is to create a hybrid system with the UNSW-NB15
dataset, in which machine learning models carry out intrusion detection, and LLMs
provide explanations. Besides, two LLMs will be fine-tuned using LoRA, to enhance
theirperformanceondatarelatedtocybersecurity,allowingamorepreciseandcontext-
sensitiveanalysis.

8.1 CONCLUSION
This project proposed and implemented a hybrid cybersecurity threat detection frame-
work that combines the strengths of traditional Machine Learning (ML) models with
the reasoning capabilities of Large Language Models (LLMs). The objective of the
systemwastoaccuratelydetectmaliciousnetworkactivity,classifythreatseverity,and
generateexplainableoutputssuitableforreal-worldcybersecurityenvironments.
ThesystemwasevaluatedusingtheUNSW-NB15dataset,whichprovidesarealistic
representation of modern network traffic containing both benign and malicious activ-
ities. A comprehensive preprocessing pipeline was designed to clean, transform, and
preparethedataforbothstructuredML-basedanalysisandtext-basedLLMinference.
Onthemachinelearningside,threemodelswereimplementedandevaluated: Ran-
dom Forest, XGBoost, and Isolation Forest. Among these, XGBoost achieved the
highest overall performance with an accuracy of 90.56%, an F1-score of 91.86%, and
an ROC-AUC of 0.984. Random Forest demonstrated exceptionally high recall, mak-
ing it suitable in environments where minimizing false negatives is critical. Isolation
Forest, while useful as an anomaly detection baseline, showed significantly lower per-
formance, highlighting the limitations of purely unsupervised approaches on complex
moderndatasets.
On the LLM side, three instruction-tuned models—Phi-4 Mini, Llama-3.2 3B, and
Gemma-2 2B—were evaluated in both zero-shot and QLoRA fine-tuned settings. The
zero-shot models demonstrated limited domain-specific understanding, with severity
classification accuracies ranging between 32% and 48.5%. However, after applying
Quantized Low-Rank Adaptation (QLoRA), substantial improvements were observed
acrossallmodels.
Among the evaluated LLMs, Phi-4 Mini achieved the best performance after fine-
tuning, reaching a severity classification accuracy of 92% while maintaining a 100%
valid JSON output rate. This demonstrates that parameter-efficient fine-tuning can
significantlyimprovetheeffectivenessandreliabilityofLLMsincybersecurity-specific

The results clearly show that traditional machine learning models excel in fast and
accurate binary intrusion detection, while fine-tuned LLMs provide strong reasoning,
explanation generation, and structured threat analysis. By integrating both approaches,
the proposed framework offers a balanced solution that is accurate, explainable, and
scalable.
Overall,thisprojectsuccessfullydemonstratesthepracticalfeasibilityofdeploying
